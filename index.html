    About Us

    We are a team of clinicians, engineers, and innovators working at the intersection of medicine and immersive technology.

    XR-Guided Lung Tumor Localization

    Our mission is to make precise, real-time tumor localization more accessible, intuitive, and infrastructure-light. By integrating extended reality (XR), 3D medical imaging, and clinical insight, we aim to transform how surgeons plan and perform complex procedures—starting with lung tumor resections.

    We believe that:
    • Advanced surgical guidance should not require expensive infrastructure.
    • Every surgeon—junior or experienced—deserves spatial clarity and decision support.
    • Better preoperative planning leads to safer, faster, and more confident surgery.

    Our XR-guided system is already helping thoracic teams visualize tumor positions, plan safe needle paths, and simulate minimally invasive approaches—all before stepping into the OR.

    Immersive 3D VR Anatomy & Surgery Learning

    The OpVerse Immersive 3D VR Anatomy & Surgery Learning Platform transforms traditional medical education into an interactive, patient-specific experience. By combining extended reality (XR) with high-fidelity 3D reconstructions from medical imaging, we create a virtual operating room where students and clinicians can explore, manipulate, and rehearse complex anatomy and surgical procedures in unprecedented detail.

    Core Values

    The OpVerse platform delivers an immersive learning experience that brings anatomy and surgical training into a virtual operating room. With VR/AR headsets, learners can freely explore organs and pathologies from every angle, strengthening their spatial understanding in a way that textbooks and 2D images cannot. By reconstructing patient-specific 3D models from CT and MRI data, the system also enables personalized planning and simulation, ensuring that every training session or preoperative rehearsal reflects real clinical challenges.

    Beyond exploration, the platform emphasizes interactive practice, allowing users to zoom, rotate, dissect, and annotate structures while rehearsing actual surgical steps. This hands-on engagement reinforces anatomical knowledge and builds procedural confidence. Designed for collaboration, it supports cross-disciplinary education by bringing together medical students, residents, radiologists, and engineers in the same XR space. Finally, its portability and cost-effectiveness make advanced surgical education accessible anywhere—requiring only a standard PC and an XR device, without the need for cadavers, labs, or costly hybrid operating rooms.

    Designed For

    • Medical Students & Residents: Build 3D anatomical intuition and confidence faster.
    • Clinical Teams: Collaboratively plan safe surgical strategies pre-operatively.
    • Academic & Training Centers: Deliver next-generation, interactive teaching and remote education.

    Impact

    Already adopted in teaching hospitals, OpVerse VR training has been shown to shorten the learning curve, improve surgical preparedness, and enhance confidence in complex procedures. By bridging medical expertise with immersive technology, we are redefining surgical education — from static learning to dynamic, interactive, and immersive training experiences.

    VATS Port Planning

    The OpVerse VATS Port Planning Module empowers surgeons to design and evaluate incision strategies for video-assisted thoracoscopic surgery (VATS). By leveraging extended reality (XR) with patient-specific 3D reconstructions, our platform simulates multiple port placements, helping surgeons choose the most ergonomic and effective approach to optimize visibility, instrument access, and patient recovery.

    Core Values

    Effective port placement is critical for successful minimally invasive thoracic surgery. The OpVerse system allows surgeons to virtually test different incision sites on a patient’s 3D model before entering the operating room. This process improves preoperative planning by clarifying anatomical relationships, evaluating instrument angles, and ensuring that chosen entry points maximize visibility while minimizing risk. By simulating multiple strategies, surgical teams can select the safest and most efficient configuration tailored to each case.

    Beyond planning, our platform enhances ergonomic assessment and team training. Surgeons can anticipate instrument reach and hand positioning, while residents learn best practices in a safe, interactive environment. This collaborative XR space fosters discussion between attending surgeons, trainees, and supporting staff, aligning the entire team around a well-tested operative plan. By lowering the trial-and-error factor, VATS procedures become more predictable, efficient, and confidence-driven.

    Designed For

    • Thoracic Surgeons: Optimize incision placement for better visibility, safety, and outcomes.
    • Surgical Residents: Practice port planning in an XR environment before entering the OR.
    • Surgical Teams: Collaboratively evaluate ergonomic setups and coordinate operative strategies.

    Impact

    With OpVerse VATS Port Planning, hospitals can reduce operative uncertainty, shorten setup time, and improve ergonomics for minimally invasive thoracic procedures. By bridging patient-specific imaging with immersive simulation, our system enables safer, more efficient surgeries—empowering teams to move from reactive adjustments in the OR to proactive, well-planned strategies that improve patient recovery and surgical confidence.

    Needle Path Planning

    The OpVerse Needle Path Planning Module provides clinicians with an immersive XR environment to design and validate preoperative dye injection or biopsy trajectories. By reconstructing patient-specific 3D anatomy, the platform helps surgeons plan safe, accurate routes that avoid critical structures such as vessels, bronchi, and ribs—improving precision and patient safety.

    Core Values

    Accurate needle placement is one of the most delicate challenges in thoracic surgery. The OpVerse system enhances spatial awareness and risk reduction by allowing surgeons to visualize potential needle paths directly within a 3D model of the patient. With clear visibility of tumor location relative to surrounding anatomy, clinicians can identify the safest and most direct route to the target while avoiding vital structures. This capability improves confidence during planning and minimizes the chance of intraoperative surprises.

    Beyond planning, the module supports training and collaboration. Junior clinicians can rehearse different trajectory options and immediately understand the implications of each choice, while senior surgeons and multidisciplinary teams use the same XR space to align on the optimal strategy. Because the solution is portable and infrastructure-light, hospitals can adopt it widely without the need for expensive imaging suites—making precise preoperative planning accessible to more institutions and patients.

    Designed For

    • Interventional Radiologists & Thoracic Surgeons: Plan safe and precise injection or biopsy routes.
    • Residents & Trainees: Practice trajectory design in a controlled XR environment with immediate feedback.
    • Multidisciplinary Teams: Collaborate on patient-specific strategies to reduce risks and improve outcomes.

    Impact

    With OpVerse Needle Path Planning, clinicians can increase accuracy, reduce complications, and build confidence in delicate preoperative interventions. By transforming 2D imaging into intuitive 3D simulation, the system empowers surgical teams to move from estimation to evidence-based planning—delivering safer, faster, and more reliable procedures for every patient.
