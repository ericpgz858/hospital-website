Needle Path Planning with XR Guidance

During preoperative dye injection, clinicians must navigate around vital structures such as blood vessels and bones to reach the tumor site. Our system enables intuitive route planning in a 3D XR environment, helping physicians visualize the safest and most direct path to the lesion—reducing risk and increasing confidence.

Read More

VATS Port Planning

For thoracoscopic or minimally invasive surgery, the location of incisions is critical for visibility, instrument maneuverability, and patient recovery. Our platform allows surgeons to simulate multiple port placements in a 3D patient-specific model, helping evaluate workspace feasibility and choose the most ergonomic and effective entry points.

    Accurate, Intuitive Tumor Localization

    Our XR platform provides a real-time, spatially-anchored visualization of lung tumors in 3D, enabling surgeons to precisely understand tumor positions relative to patient anatomy—even before the incision.

    Without Hybrid OR Dependency

    Traditionally, tumor localization relies on costly intraoperative imaging and complex hybrid OR setups. We eliminate that dependency, making precision-guided surgery accessible to more hospitals and patients.

    Lower the Learning Curve for Surgical Trainees

    XR guidance helps young residents and junior surgeons rapidly develop spatial understanding and procedural confidence. Complex lung resections become more approachable, structured, and teachable.

    Reduced Equipment Cost, Increased Surgical Confidence

    Empower surgical teams with enhanced spatial awareness and reduce the need for real-time imaging or navigational hardware. Optimize resource use while maintaining precision.

    About Us

    We are a team of clinicians, engineers, and innovators working at the intersection of medicine and immersive technology.

    XR-Guided Lung Tumor Localization

    Our mission is to make precise, real-time tumor localization more accessible, intuitive, and infrastructure-light. By integrating extended reality (XR), 3D medical imaging, and clinical insight, we aim to transform how surgeons plan and perform complex procedures—starting with lung tumor resections.

    We believe that:
    • Advanced surgical guidance should not require expensive infrastructure.
    • Every surgeon—junior or experienced—deserves spatial clarity and decision support.
    • Better preoperative planning leads to safer, faster, and more confident surgery.

    Our XR-guided system is already helping thoracic teams visualize tumor positions, plan safe needle paths, and simulate minimally invasive approaches—all before stepping into the OR.
